Known weaknesses

  • Anglophone bias. Coverage is journal-whitelist driven; non-English flagships are under-represented.
  • Solo-influence undercount. Co-authorship inference misses Coleman, Granovetter, Tilly — scholars who trained students by reading-and-conversation, not co-writing.
  • Pre-1980 disambiguation noise. OpenAlex's record-linking is uncertain for older publications; some pre-1980 trees absorb name collisions.
  • Sparse recent students. Famous mentors have 40+ named PhD students on CVs, but many haven't accumulated enough citations yet to land in the universe. They'll show up in 5–10 years.
  • Verified edges = 211. Only ~5% of edges are Wikipedia-verified; the rest are Sinatra-asymmetry-inferred. Verified edges are the gold standard; inferred edges are the hum-level approximation.
