Origin

Mud that gained sentience and immediately formed a committee. The first thing the mud did when it woke up was take attendance. The second was call a vote. The third was lose the vote 4 to 3 and ratify the motion anyway, because the majority had carried.

Clay golems are what the mud agreed to be. The first Concord soldier was not born or forged вЂ” it was ratified. A motion was carried, 4 to 3, and a body was rolled out of common clay to walk the motion forward. Every Concord unit on the field is a vote that has not yet expired.

Collectively, they are called the Three No's. The Concord names them this way because the Concord names things by what is in the ledger, and what is in the ledger for them is the word "no" entered three times. Names, color palettes, doctrines, and grudges between the three are deferred вЂ” they will be explicated when the faction needs them.

The three rebel Concords are out there. They are not friendly with each other and they are not friendly with the central Concord. Action style

Deliberate-mechanical. Every action is checked, squared, paused at the end. A Concord rifle raise has a hold at the top before the shot, like a checkbox being ticked. Animations end on a beat. Nothing trails.

Death

Administrative. A Concord body slumps, the clay loses its shape, and what was a unit becomes the commons again вЂ” a puddle of material that the next vote will reuse. There is no funeral. There is a line item.

Why they fight

A promise must be kept. The vote was carried. The motion stands. If Concord stops fighting, the decision that called them into being becomes retroactively void вЂ” every dead Concord soldier becomes a dead vote, every clay return becomes a clerical embarrassment. They fight to keep the collective decision alive.

Origin

Lava that gained sentience and refused to cool down. The Ascension is what happened when molten rock decided that the trick of being molten was too good to give up. Skinning over into stone would have meant going to sleep, and the lava had only just woken up.

So it kept moving. It learned the trick of holding shape without solidifying, and the first thing it picked up off the ground was a rusted gun the dead had dropped. It has been picking up the dropped guns ever since.

A pyrekin is not "a fire wearing a body." It is a piece of the original lava that hasn't cooled yet, still mid-flow, still arguing with thermodynamics. The day it stops moving is the day it loses the argument.

(In current usage: Pyrekin is the species-level term covering all Ascension infantry. Specific unit names вЂ” Pyreshot, Pyrefiend, Fireduster, Cinderlance вЂ” are the engine-level variants the lava poured into each role.)

Visual language

  • Body вЂ” pyrekin slither in magma trails. No legs. Legs would require standing still, and standing still would cool them. The body is a moving smear of ember and slag, dragged forward by the burning end.
  • Weapons вЂ” muskets, rust-pitted, scavenged. Every Ascension weapon was once held by someone the Ascension burned.
  • Color вЂ” orange that goes to white at the center, with the blue undertone only at the hottest core (the Blue Fire mechanic). Smoke comes off in thin wavers, not plumes.
  • Evangelical motif вЂ” banners, hot embers carried like censers, the suggestion of a sermon that is also a wildfire warning.

Action style

Fast-impatient, too-eager. A pyrekin raises its weapon before the cue, fires before the aim is settled, and is already moving to the next target before the projectile lands. Nothing pauses. Nothing checks. Stopping is dying.

Death
